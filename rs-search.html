Search & Filter Experience

The ability to search and filter support tickets are one of the key functionalities for a majority of the help desk and support products. Usable search mechanisms help users find valuable information in a sea of data so that they feel in control.

As common and natural as it is to have search functionality available to users, sometimes we lack consistency and conditions that actually make a feature usable.

To provide a predictable and consistent experience familiar to users we decided to redesign our mobile applications search & filtering experience.

  • Role: Lead Experience & Interface Designer
  • Platforms: Mobile Android & iOS
  • Date: February 2019

Problem

The approach customers expect to search and filter for tickets did not perform accurately to their mental models. This made their search experience ineffective and meaningless.

Research

To fully understand the issues and observe the behavior of the existing design from a user perspective, our research team invited and conducted twelve (12) interview sessions with real Rackspace customers.

Primary Issues

  • Ticketing status worked independently of search
  • Guided search forced users into a key/value approach they were unfamiliar with
  • Results are presented in pills which proved bulky and cumbersome

Recommendations

  • Make ‘Open’ tickets the default
  • Reduce ticketing categories and topics
  • Consistency between Ticketing & Notifications

Defining types of data to search & filter

The first step to creating an improved search experience was to understand the types of data available in the product.

  • Status shows all tickets with an Open, Closed or View All status
  • Accounts include only the accounts selected by the user
  • Classification refines results to include Incident, Change, Service Request or All ticket types
  • Severity sorts tickets based on Low, Normal, High, Urgent or All priorities
  • Date includes all dates and is specified by using a date picker

Defining data types

  • Search - string input
  • Status - Radio Button
  • Accounts - Checkbox List
  • Classification - Radio Button
  • Severity - Radio Button
  • Date - Date Picker

Conceptualizing Ideas

Next, was to start taking the research insights and recommendations to visually implement a search filter experience that increases our customer’s productivity. Providing a free form text search made more sense to our customers, so most of the challenge here was how to handle the filtering mechanism.

As a result, we designed a fully functioning search and filtering experience that helps users find tickets more efficiently.

Outcome

As a result, we designed a fully functioning search and filtering experience that helps users find tickets more efficiently.

  • Created clear guidelines of which types of data can be filtered
  • Allows for typing keywords and strings that users identify tickets by
  • Implemented a consistent design pattern that can be consumed by other parts of the application
