New Rackspace Support Docs

Rackspace Technology customers need to have the flexibility to look for support documentation to maintain their infrastructure. Our solution should deliver an easy way for customers to share and find relevant documentation to determine if they will administer the task themselves before contacting Rackspace Support.

  • Role: Lead Experience & Interface Designer
  • Platforms: Web and Mobile Responsive
  • Project Length: September 2020 - January 2021 (4 Months)

Contributors

I led the design team efforts where I collaborated and worked with 2 designers, 1 researcher, 3 content editors and 2 engineers. Helping to discover the real problem(s), develop strategy, create assets and deliver the overall concept to improve the experience.

The Challenge

Our support documentation pages lacked platform and visual consistency. Additionally, it carried with it ineffective wayfinding and poor search capabilities among other things.

The Solution

A newly informative single support docs website to help customers find, trouble shoot, discover and stay up to date on the latest news around the technology they use.

Discovery

We conducted interviews, performed heuristic and competitive analysis to understand pain points our customers faced when looking for support documentation. Here are the key takeaways:

  • Two navigation structures
  • Terminology varies site-to-site
  • Inconsistent visual design
  • Inconsistent interaction design
  • Poor Search capabilities
  • Ineffective wayfinding

Analysis

These insights made us aware that our customers were extremely confused and we needed to make it easier for them to find, search, and get the information they were looking for.

Our research and content teams worked diligently to remodel the information architecture, nomenclature and categorization of products and services. Meanwhile, the design team dove deep into heuristic evaluations, navigation, footer and competitive analysis.

Through this process, we discovered that we need to provide the following:

  • Search & Results
  • Clear Categories of Documentation
  • Usage of visuals to break up content
  • Use of left navigation to display documentation
  • Feedback mechanism within the page of the article
  • Usage of a right column for additional information and actions
  • Breadcrumbs for wayfinding

Explore

With organized information architecture and product categorization our team was ready to discuss and explore. We started with user stories to guide our design decisions and it gets exciting.

  • As a Rackspace Technology customer, I want to be able to try solving problems on my own so that I do not have to contact Rackspace Support.
  • As a user, I want to know what are the tasks I can comfortably do on my own so that I do not have to contact support.
  • As a developer, I want to be able to find API documentation and SDKs so that I can learn how to set up my environment with Rackspace Technology.
  • As a Rackspace customer, I want to read articles from experts in the industry so that I can advance my technical skills.

This gave us the confidence to begin creating wireframes and low fidelity concepts of how a newly imagined experience could look like.

Testing & Validation

We tested our concepts internally with our research and content groups, along with a couple designers with no direct relationship to the project. Reactions were mostly positive and we were able to take feedback and apply updates quickly and effectively.

Implementation

The validation let us know we could get started on the redesign. Starting with the basic and primary pages to quickly handoff to developers so that they could begin. High fidelity design work was delivered via Abstract. Design efforts were then distributed to remake Technical Docs, Expert Insights, Responsive Navigation Menu, and Search.

At every step, I would communicate and schedule demo reviews with engineers to ensure designs were implemented to spec. Very positive and healthy design/developer relationship that I’ve been a part of.

Impact

The rollout of a new Support Docs site now aligns to the Rackspace Technology brand, consolidating top level navigation and footers. Utilizing a framework throughout the site for consistency allowing topics to be more visible as the user navigates. We’ve included helpful wayfinding mechanisms, consistent branding and improved search & search results.
